Analysis of Results

The DCF model suggests AAPL trades above its discounted cash flow value, which aligns with the common observation that Apple commands a significant brand/ecosystem premium. The wide CCA range ($22–$276) reflects diversity within big-tech peers (Amazon's lower margins vs Meta's higher margins produce very different implied multiples). The Precedent Transactions range sits higher than CCA, correctly reflecting control premiums in tech M&A. The LOW confidence flag is appropriate — big-tech companies are notoriously hard to value from fundamentals alone due to intangible brand value.

Hardening Changes Made During Evaluation

Issues discovered and fixed during real-world testing:

  1. Incomplete fiscal year filtering (fetch_data.py) — Yahoo Finance returns oldest years with all-null financials. Now auto-filters records where revenue is null. Eliminated 27 warnings on AAPL.
  2. Feature engineering null guard (feature_engineering.py) — Skips records where both revenue and total_assets are null, preventing division errors.
  3. Invalid ticker graceful skip (fetch_data.py) — Yahoo Finance 404 errors caught and turned into skip-with-warning, not a crash.
  4. Post-filter peer count re-check — After filtering, re-validates that ≥3 peers with valid data remain.
